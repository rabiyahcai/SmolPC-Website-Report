SmolPC

Abstract

The Problem

Artificial intelligence is rapidly transforming education, yet access to AI-powered learning tools remains deeply unequal. Most mainstream systems still assume reliable internet access, cloud accounts, and setup steps that are poorly suited to real classroom deployment. As a result, the students and teachers who could benefit most from AI support are often the least able to use it. Schools need software that is private, locally run, easier to deploy, and able to make sensible use of the hardware already available to them, while still supporting the everyday tasks students do across writing, coding, image editing, and 3D design.

Our Platform

SmolPC is a free, open-source, offline AI assistant platform for education, with CodeHelper as its flagship experience and integrated assistants for LibreOffice, GIMP, and Blender. The project began from an earlier LibreOffice-based SmolPC prototype, but evolved into a broader cross-platform system built with Tauri, Rust, and Svelte as the scope expanded. As deployment requirements became clearer, we moved away from an Ollama-centred setup towards a shared local inference and orchestration layer that can recognise the hardware and software environment available on a device and select the best supported local runtime path for it, while still keeping the full experience local and privacy-preserving.

Our Impact: Education for All

SmolPC demonstrates that practical local AI for education does not have to depend on cloud services or complex per-device setup. With CodeHelper at the core of a broader unified platform spanning coding, writing, image editing, and 3D workflows, the project aims to make local AI more usable in real classrooms. Following consultation with teachers through our supervisor, Professor Mohamedally, and a demonstration to the National Autistic Society, feedback has been overwhelmingly positive, with educators expressing strong interest in adopting SmolPC in their classrooms. SmolPC was also showcased at the BETT Conference, one of the world's leading educational technology events, where it was met with enthusiasm from educators and institutions alike. We hope to see SmolPC deployed in schools globally, giving every student access to an AI learning platform that supports them across coding, writing, design, and beyond.
